# 03 — Industry-Intelligence Landscape (Production Atlas)

**For:** Production Atlas (live-event industry intelligence & opportunity research —
**not** a job board).
**Question:** Who maps the live-event industry / connects crew to work, and how is
Production Atlas different?
**Researched:** 2026-07-21 (web).

## Bottom line

Two adjacent spaces are occupied — **job-board/directory marketplaces**
(ProductionHUB, Giggs) and **premium touring data** (Pollstar) — but **neither
does Production Atlas's actual thing**: worker-facing, public-safe, *structured
intelligence* on employer ecosystems, department pathways, and regional
production opportunity. The competition is more apparent than real, but these
incumbents define what Production Atlas must **not** try to be.

## Direct analogs — marketplaces / directories

- **ProductionHUB** — the largest global network of film/video/live-event pros;
  **150,000+ verified profiles**, a directory (incl. live-events staging & rigging)
  **and** a jobs board. A **marketplace** (profiles + postings), not an
  intelligence map.
- **Giggs** (giggs.live) — "#1 community for live event professionals"; connects
  pros with employers. Community + jobs.

**Difference:** both are two-sided marketplaces where people list themselves and
employers post gigs. Production Atlas is a **curated map** of *who produces/routes
what*, department pathways, and regional opportunity — research, not a listings
exchange.

## The big incumbent — premium data

- **Pollstar** — the leading touring-industry data resource: box office, routing,
  directories, a live-entertainment contact database, **319,000+ artists**, 43
  years of data, predictive analytics. **Owned by Live Nation since 2013.**

**Difference:** Pollstar is **exec/promoter/agent-facing** and about
**artists, tours, and money**. Production Atlas is **worker-facing** and about
**crew, departments, employers, and where the work is** — the opposite end of the
same industry. Pollstar is a benchmark and a "don't compete here," not a partner
(Live Nation-owned, expensive).

## Strategic implications (Production Atlas's play)

- **Stay out of the job-board fight.** You already decided Atlas is *not* a job
  board — this research confirms that lane is taken (ProductionHUB/Giggs). Your
  edge is **structured, verified intelligence**, not listings.
- **Own the worker's-eye map.** Nobody offers a public-safe, department/pathway,
  regional-opportunity map built *for the crew person*. That's the whitespace.
- **Possible data/referral partners** (not competitors): ProductionHUB or Giggs
  (cross-referral / data), and **IATSE** (pathways + credibility). Pollstar =
  study, don't chase.

## Partner leads (for `connections.md`)

- **ProductionHUB** — data/referral or cross-promotion candidate.
- **Giggs** — community cross-promotion.
- **IATSE (locals / national)** — pathways, credibility, worker reach (also a Crew
  Blueprint partner — one relationship, two businesses).
