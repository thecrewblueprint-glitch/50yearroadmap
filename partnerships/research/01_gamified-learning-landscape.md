# 01 — Gamified Learning Landscape (Stagehand / Rigging / Production)

**For:** The Crew Blueprint (gamified static delivery, `cb-3`/`cb-5`).
**Question:** Who has *officially gamified* learning for stagehands, live-event
production, and rigging in live music — and how did they do it?
**Researched:** 2026-07-21 (web).

## Bottom line

The exact niche — **gamified training for professional live-music stagehands,
with real rigging depth** — is **essentially open.** One close analog exists
(**Tech Crew HQ**, Australia), the gamified-trades *model* is proven at scale in
**other** trades (**Interplay Learning**, **Transfr** — 500k+ / 330+ sims), and
the live-event training field is full of **traditional, non-gamified** players who
are better read as **content/credibility partners than competitors.** That
combination — a proven pattern, one distant analog, and a credibility-rich field
with no gamified pro-rigging offering — is a strong opening for Crew Blueprint.

## Tier 1 — Direct analog: **Tech Crew HQ** (Australia)

The closest thing to Crew Blueprint that exists today.

- **What:** an online platform teaching backstage production skills with
  "interactive elements like games, interactive boards, quizzes and practical
  activities." Built by industry practitioners ("Inspired Productions").
- **Covers:** lighting, audio/sound, vision/video (projectors, LED, cameras,
  switchers), stage management, safety, careers.
- **Audience:** "high-school and above"; explicitly aimed at **schools, colleges,
  community theatre, arts orgs** — the *education* market.
- **Gamification:** real but **light** — games/quizzes/interactive boards; no
  visible badges/points/leaderboards/skill-tree ("gamification appears minimal").
- **Business model:** cheap **per-course** (AUD ~$10 Backstage Essentials, ~$60
  full, ~$15 specialty), plus custom in-person workshops. No subscription or
  institutional licensing surfaced.
- **Curriculum backing:** markets itself as practitioner-built; a Google summary
  tied it to the Australian **VET Certificate III in Live Production**, but the
  site itself doesn't claim formal accreditation.

**What it proves:** a gamified backstage-crew training product is real and viable.
**Where Crew Blueprint differs (the opening):**
1. **Rigging** — Tech Crew HQ **does not cover rigging.** Rigging is Crew
   Blueprint's *strongest* asset (publication-ready packets, ETCP alignment).
2. **Audience** — they serve students/community theatre; Crew Blueprint targets
   **working professional live-music crew** (touring, festivals, arenas).
3. **Depth of gamification** — theirs is light; Crew Blueprint's skill-tree + real
   interactions can go deeper.
4. **Geography** — they're AU-focused; Crew Blueprint is US live-music. → more a
   **potential partner/licensor than a competitor.**

## Tier 2 — Proven model to emulate (different trades)

These validate the *mechanics* Crew Blueprint chose, at scale:

- **Interplay Learning** — the benchmark. VR + 3D simulation for skilled trades
  (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, maintenance). **500,000+ workers trained.**
  Explicit gamification: **points, badges, leaderboards** + hands-on sim practice.
  Sells to employers and education.
- **Transfr** — hands-on **VR** training + career exploration; 330+ sims across 8
  sectors; research-backed engagement/retention.
- Industry write-ups (e.g. ABC Carolinas) treat **gamified skilled-trades
  training** as a workforce-development strategy.

**Lesson:** the "gamified trades training" pattern works and scales — nobody has
pointed it at live-event/entertainment rigging. Interplay's points/badges/sim
loop is the closest template for Crew Blueprint's node mechanics.

## Tier 3 — The field (traditional; best seen as partners)

Established live-event/rigging training — credible, but **not gamified**. These
are Crew Blueprint's **content/credibility/distribution partner candidates**
(playbook Track 3), not competitors:

- **Academy of Live Technology @ Rock Lititz** (with PCA&D; UK Production Park
  roots as "Backstage Academy") — accredited **BFA in Live Event Design**, working
  campus, partners incl. Clair Global, TAIT, ATOMIC. Major US hub.
- **ITI** (iti.com) — online **entertainment rigging** courses **and** a
  1,000+-scenario **VR crane simulator** (entertainment-adjacent VR exists here).
- **Bigger Hammer** — ETCP-recognized practical safety (forklift, MEWP, fall
  protection, ground rigging).
- **Rugged Training Group** — stagehand, forklift, live-production training;
  online + on-site.
- **USITT** — virtual on-the-job rigging training.
- **Superior Stage Crew** — 2-day rigging bootcamp.
- **ETCP (ESTA)** — the certification *standard* Crew Blueprint aligns to (Rigger
  Arena/Theatre, Electrician). Not a course provider; the credential bar.
- **Purdue Fusion Studio** — online structural design/analysis for live
  entertainment. Colleges: MHCC, Irvine Valley College; **CITT** (Canada).

## Adjacent tech

- **VR rigging/crane simulation** (ITI; NACB/CraneSafe; academic work — Firenze,
  MDPI) demonstrates VR + gamified hazard/lift-path training works. Heavier than
  Crew Blueprint's static approach, but a future option if a real need appears.

## Strategic implications (the whitespace)

Crew Blueprint's defensible position = the intersection **no one occupies**:
**professional live-music audience × real rigging depth × genuine gamification ×
US market.** Tech Crew HQ owns none of that intersection; the field owns
credibility but not gamification; Interplay/Transfr own gamification but not the
industry.

- **Lead with rigging** — it's the clearest gap and your strongest content.
- **Emulate Interplay's mechanics** (points/badges/progress + one real
  interaction), not an LMS — consistent with the `cb-3` decision.
- **Position pro, not academic** — the working-crew angle differentiates from Tech
  Crew HQ and the college programs.

## Partner leads (candidates for `connections.md`)

- **Tech Crew HQ** — possible **content-share / licensing / rigging-module**
  partner (they lack rigging; complementary geography). Watch + explore.
- **Academy of Live Technology / Rock Lititz** — credibility + reach; high-value,
  harder to land.
- **Bigger Hammer**, **Rugged Training Group**, **ITI** — ETCP-aligned trainers;
  co-branded/endorsement candidates.
- **ETCP/ESTA** — alignment/credibility relationship (standards body).
