# 02 — Labor-Staffing Landscape (Deadhang Labor)

**For:** Deadhang Labor LLC (solo → crews → labor-staffing).
**Question:** Who runs live-event stagehand labor-staffing, how did they scale, and
are they competitors or partners?
**Researched:** 2026-07-21 (web).

## Bottom line

This is a **mature, competitive, national market** — Deadhang is a tiny solo
player in it. But the key insight is that these companies are **simultaneously
competitors and Deadhang's best partners**: Deadhang already works *through* some
of them (Upstage as a 1099 client; Crew One appears in the portfolio). That
directly validates the playbook's **Track 1** — don't try to out-scale them,
become their **preferred subcontractor crew**.

## The field (national staffing companies)

- **Rhino Staging** — bills itself as the **leading national** event-labor staffing
  provider; skilled stagehand crews across the US.
- **Crew One Productions** — nationwide; stagehands, riggers, spotlight ops.
  Non-union (pay/benefits differ from union scale). *(Already in Deadhang's orbit —
  portfolio.)*
- **Upstage Companies** — trained stagehands + general labor for concerts,
  corporate, festivals. *(Deadhang's existing 1099 relationship — the warmest
  partner lead.)*
- **Show Support USA** — event crew coordination, ops-team communication.
- **Stage Crew®** — full-spectrum: A/V techs, riggers, electricians, carpenters,
  stagehands, loaders, operators.
- **ShowPros / ShoPros** — skilled labor & staffing for live events.

## How they scaled (the "how they did it" lesson)

The pattern is consistent: **start local → build a reliable, vetted roster →
earn a reputation for reliability + safety → expand regionally → add
workforce-management software** (LASSO / Nowsta / Rentman, as your own business
plan noted) to schedule/dispatch/invoice at scale. The revenue model is the
standard labor-broker **markup on contractor pay** (≈30–50%).

## Strategic implications (Deadhang's play)

- **Partner before compete.** Your fastest growth is as a **preferred
  subcontractor / crew-lead** *under* one of these (Track 1) — they own the
  clients, insurance umbrella, and cash flow you're currently missing.
- **Warm starts, not cold.** Upstage (current relationship) and Crew One
  (portfolio) are already-open doors — start there.
- **Differentiate on the ecosystem.** Safety-first + **Crew-Blueprint-trained** +
  a defined region (AZ/Southwest) is a story a big roster-driven staffer can't
  easily tell. You're not just labor; you're reliable, trained, and building
  infrastructure.
- **The gate:** the moment you bring *others* under Deadhang, classification
  (`dh-10`) and insurance (`dh-9`) go live. Subcontracting *yourself* first
  sidesteps that.

## Partner leads (for `connections.md`)

- **Upstage Companies** — existing 1099 relationship; **warmest** — pursue first.
- **Crew One Productions** — existing portfolio connection.
- **Rhino Staging**, **Show Support USA**, **Stage Crew®** — national roster
  companies; preferred-subcontractor candidates.
