# Partnership Playbook

**What this is:** the strategic system for using partnerships to accelerate growth
across the whole ecosystem — Deadhang Labor, The Crew Blueprint, Production Atlas,
and the Homestead. It exists to turn "we should partner with people" into a
repeatable, sequenced process with a clear offer, target list, outreach, and
deal structures.

## The strategic spine (read this first)

Everything in this playbook hangs off three ideas. If a section ever contradicts
these, the section is wrong.

1. **Partnerships rent the infrastructure that's blocking you.** As a solo
   operator, your hardest constraints — demand, crew-scaling, insurance,
   credibility, capital — are things a partner has already solved. A partnership
   lets you *rent* that solution and pay for it out of margin instead of capital.

2. **Find one anchor.** The highest-leverage outcome is landing a single **anchor
   partner** (ideally a staffing or mid-size production company) who can deliver
   **labor demand + industry data + a training pipeline** from one relationship —
   validating the "Labor → Training → Jobs" flywheel and pulling all four
   businesses forward together.

3. **Sequence by leverage, not by excitement.** The order is deliberate:

   | # | Track | Why this order |
   | --- | --- | --- |
   | 1 | **Deadhang staffing partnership** | Fastest to revenue; deepens an existing relationship; dissolves demand + scaling + insurance at once; safest on 1099 (you're the subcontractor). Funds everything else. |
   | 2 | **Production Atlas data** | Run as the *same conversation* — labor partners and data sources are the same companies. One relationship, two payoffs. |
   | 3 | **Crew Blueprint training** | Credibility play; content-gated. Gets much easier once the first gamified module (`cb-5`) exists to show. |
   | 4 | **Homestead / nomadic orgs** | Long-horizon, low-bandwidth. Keep warm (Roadie Clinic already contacted); don't let it pull focus. |

## How to use it

- **Strategizing / choosing where to focus:** read 01–03.
- **Going after a specific partner:** 04 (qualify) → 05 (tailor the offer) → 06
  (reach out) → 07 (track it) → 08 (structure the deal).
- **The live target list is `data/roadmap/connections.md`** — this playbook is the
  *method*; connections.md is the *who*. Keep them in sync but don't duplicate.

## Guardrails (public-safe — repo is public)

- This playbook holds **strategy and templates**, not sensitive specifics. Keep
  OUT: individual contacts' personal details, private negotiation terms, rates,
  or anything a partner shared in confidence. Those live in private storage.
- Partner **organizations** (business names, public info) are fine — as in
  `connections.md`.
- Don't overstate what Deadhang is today. The offer must be true (a solo operator
  building toward crews), not the aspirational business plan.
