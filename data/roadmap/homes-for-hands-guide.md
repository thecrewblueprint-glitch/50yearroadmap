# Homes for Hands — Live & Travel Free

*A resource guide for the caravan — traveling live-event workers.*

This is the whole of the Homestead now: **not a place we build, but a map of what
already exists.** The US is full of free and low-cost ways to travel and rest, and
of people who help crew when they're down. This guide points you to them. It's
care and information, offered freely — nothing to buy from us, nothing to run.

> **How to use it:** everything here is run by other people and changes over time —
> **verify current details, prices, and rules before you rely on them.** Overnight
> parking and camping laws vary by place; when in doubt, ask. This is a starting
> map, not legal or safety advice.

---

## 1. Finding places to sleep (free & cheap)

- **The Dyrt** (thedyrt.com) — large US camping app: campground reviews, a
  **free-camping finder**, and offline maps + trip planning on the paid (PRO)
  tier. Good first stop for "where can I stay tonight."
- **Public land — dispersed camping** — much **BLM** land and **National Forest**
  land allows free dispersed camping (typically up to 14 days). Look up the local
  ranger district's rules.
- **Other finder apps** — iOverlander, Campendium, FreeRoam, and Recreation.gov
  (for reservable public campgrounds) round out coverage.
- **Overnight parking** — some truck stops, rest areas, and businesses (varies by
  location and law) allow a night's stay. Always confirm it's allowed and be a
  good guest.

## 2. Host networks (members stay free at hosts)

- **Harvest Hosts** (harvesthosts.com) — annual membership; park overnight **free**
  at ~9,700 host locations (farms, wineries, breweries, museums, attractions). The
  spirit is reciprocity: support the host with a purchase. Its **All Access /
  Boondockers Welcome** tier adds thousands of private-property hosts.
- **Escapees co-op parks** — member-owned parks (incl. one in Benson, AZ) with
  low-cost stays for members.

## 3. Community & belonging on the road

- **Escapees RV Club / Xscapers** (escapees.com) — a 40-year support network for
  nomads: mail-forwarding/domicile services, member discounts, and gatherings.
  **Xscapers** is the group for working-age nomads. Community, not just logistics.
- **Workamper News** (workamper.com) — jobs + community for traveling workers,
  including festival/event crew.

## 4. Work-for-stay (a bed in exchange for hours)

- **Workamper / RoverPass / campground-host gigs** — many campgrounds, parks, and
  resorts trade **on-site housing (or a free site)** for part-time work. A way to
  land somewhere between gigs without paying for it.

## 5. When you need real help (not just a campsite)

If you're broke, burned out, or have nowhere to land, these people exist for you:

- **The Roadie Clinic** (theroadieclinic.com) — a nonprofit **built for touring
  crew**: resources, peer support, and temporary housing help. The closest thing
  to a Homestead that already exists — reach out.
- **Backline** (backline.care) — free **mental-health & wellness** support for the
  live-event/music industry: a large clinician network and a support line. No cost.
- **Homes on Wheels Alliance / HOWA** (homesonwheelsalliance.org) — a nonprofit
  that helps people get *into* a livable rig and live nomadically (builds,
  emergency help, community).
- **211** (dial 211, or 211.org) — free, confidential connection to local social
  services (food, shelter, utilities, crisis help) anywhere in the US.
- **If you're in crisis:** call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). You
  matter, and help is free and immediate.

## 6. The Homes for Hands ethos

The point of all of this is simple: **crew looking out for crew.**

- **Asking for help is allowed.** Everyone on the road hits a hard stretch. Using
  these resources isn't failure — it's what they're for.
- **Offer it back when you can.** A spare site, a jump, a meal, a phone number, a
  ride to the next call. Mutual aid is the whole model.
- **Honest limits.** Homes for Hands is **information + connection + mutual aid** —
  not housing we own or a service we run. We point you to what already works and
  to the people who can actually help. That's the promise, and we keep it.
