## Roadmap-Relevant Decisions

### Homes for Hands Way-Station Model

**Decision:** Homes for Hands should function as a welcoming respite space for travelers and people needing rest, whether or not they are stagehands.

**Roadmap effect:** Do not over-narrow Homes for Hands to only stagehands, even though stagehands are the first and strongest service population.

### Temporary vs. Permanent Residents

**Decision:** Most people who stay at the homestead will not be permanent residents. Some will receive resources, rest, and support, then move on. Permanent residents are those who want the deeper life of the project and align with it.

**Roadmap effect:** The roadmap should distinguish between:

- transient guests / way-station users
- recurring community members
- inner-core permanent residents
- stewards

### Minimalist Turnover / Shared Care

**Decision:** Care for the physical space should be part of the culture. People should leave the place as they found it. Many guests may use RVs, tents, utilities, or simple spaces rather than formal indoor housing.

**Roadmap effect:** The service model should include low-friction, shared-space maintenance expectations rather than overbuilt room-turnover operations.

### Teaching Before Exclusion

**Decision:** If someone violates the space, the first response is to teach the why behind the standard. If the disruption is destructive and the person is not sincere in making it right, they are no longer welcome unless they sincerely repair the harm.

**Roadmap effect:** Governance should use education first, but include firm re-entry and exclusion boundaries for destructive behavior.

### Guardrails Instead of Forced Belief

**Decision:** The system cannot force people to think or feel the same way. It should create operational guardrails that guide truth, care, responsibility, and Watch/Do/Teach practice without turning the culture into a rigid manual.

**Roadmap effect:** The roadmap should define guardrails, not over-prescriptive belief-control procedures.

### Human Interaction Is the Main Habitat Risk

**Decision:** Sacred space is easier to maintain with one steward; adding people introduces risk through conflicting ideas, emotions, personalities, and behaviors. Slow, careful growth matters.

**Roadmap effect:** Early Homes for Hands growth should be deliberately slow and selection-aware.

### Deadhang Labor as a Fluid Proving Ground

**Decision:** The jobsite can be one variable in evaluating alignment. How a person works and carries themselves in the field can reveal whether they may be a good fit for deeper community participation.

**Roadmap effect:** The Deadhang-to-Homestead relationship should remain fluid and intuitive rather than becoming a rigid requirement.

### Time-Layer Milestones

**Decision:** The roadmap should use milestones such as 1 year, 5 years, 10 years, 20 years, 30 years, and 40 years, or another system that makes sense. The long-range vision exists, but the short-term layer must now be defined.

**Roadmap effect:** Create layered planning horizons rather than one flat 50-year plan.

### Build What Can Be Built Now

**Decision:** The Crew Blueprint should be built far before the physical Homes for Hands structure. The current focus should be what can be done now, not what cannot be built yet.

**Roadmap effect:** Tier 1 should prioritize:

- The Crew Blueprint technical education system
- Deadhang Labor operational maturity
- Production Atlas / resource infrastructure
- personal workflow and roadmap structure

### Roadmap DNA vs. Crew Blueprint Toolkit

**Decision:** Frequency, principles, Watch/Do/Teach philosophy, and the deeper “why” belong in the 50-year roadmap repository, not inside The Crew Blueprint curriculum.

**Roadmap effect:** Keep The Crew Blueprint professionally focused and technical. Keep the spiritual/mission principles in the roadmap/deep-context layer.

### The Crew Blueprint Technical Scope

**Decision:** The Crew Blueprint should stay focused on stagehand, rigging, AV, and production/live-music industry materials.

**Roadmap effect:** Do not turn The Crew Blueprint into the full Homes for Hands philosophy or a broad general-lifestyle platform.

### Future Worker Categories

**Decision:** The system should not become permanently limiting. Other nomadic worker categories such as security guards, bartenders, vendors, and similar travelers may be added years later, but stagehands remain the current top-tier service population.

**Roadmap effect:** Design for future extensibility while keeping the immediate build focused on stagehands and production workers.

### Crew Blueprint Discipline Axis

**Decision:** The Crew Blueprint should include granular pathways for specific jobs in the field, not only broad categories.

**Potential pathways captured:**

- Stagehand / Ground Labor
- Rigging & Steel
- Staging & Scaffolding
- Lighting
- Audio
- Video
- Production / site support roles later as needed

**Roadmap effect:** Stagehand/Ground Labor must be represented as its own discipline because ground labor is a large share of the workforce.
