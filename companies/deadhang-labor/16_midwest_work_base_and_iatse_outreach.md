# 16 — Midwest Work Base & IATSE Outreach (mobile-work plan)

**Status:** Digest of a handoff summary (owner, 2026-07-22). Companion to
[doc 15](15_business_travel_vehicle_deductions.md) (travel/vehicle tax). Together
these two are the emerging **"mobile operations / travel-for-work" plan branch.**

**Goal:** a stable / semi-stable **Midwest work base** where the owner can
**vanlife / camp while working stagehand & live-event production** calls.

## Priorities (what "good" looks like)

- Stable or semi-stable work.
- **Minimal commute** from vanlife parking/camping to jobsites.
- Midwest location.
- Best use of existing **stagehand / hand / rigging-support / live-event labor**
  experience.
- **Lower friction than downtown Chicago** (parking, traffic, density).

## The decisive factor — relationships over market size

A personal connection inside an **IATSE local** — especially a **head rigger** —
is worth more than cold-applying in a bigger market. The owner has: an
**Indianapolis IATSE** contact, and a **Grand Rapids IATSE Local 26 head-rigger**
contact. That reshaped the ranking.

## City ranking (relationship-weighted)

1. **Grand Rapids / West Michigan (IATSE Local 26)** — best balance of vanlife
   logistics, low city friction, and **relationship-based access** to calls.
2. **Indianapolis** — potentially stronger **year-round stability** if the Indy
   contact can help land work.
3. **Milwaukee / Kenosha** — backup market.
4. **Chicago** — only for **high-paying targeted calls**; hardest for vanlife
   (parking, traffic, dense-city restrictions).

> *Original ranking, before the connections were known:* Milwaukee/Kenosha/Racine ›
> Indianapolis › Madison/Janesville/Rockford corridor › Chicago suburbs ›
> Minneapolis/St. Paul. The IATSE connections moved Grand Rapids to the top.

## Outreach — draft message to the Local 26 head-rigger contact

> "I'm looking to base out of the Midwest in my van and work steadily. I'm
> reliable, available, and willing to take hand calls, rigging support,
> load-ins/load-outs, arena work, convention work, and whatever helps me get
> established. If I came to Grand Rapids for 30–60 days, how realistic is it that I
> could get consistent calls, and who else should I talk to?"

**Follow-up questions to ask:**

- Is there a slow season I should avoid?
- Would it help if I sent my resume and availability?
- Could Grand Rapids keep me busy enough, or should I split time with Indy?
- Are there venues, shops, or production companies I should contact directly?
- Any issue with me being **van-based** while taking calls, as long as I'm reliable
  and on time?

## Decision rule (which city becomes the first landing zone)

- **Local 26 gives a clear path to work → Grand Rapids** is the first landing zone.
- **Indy connection gives clearer year-round call volume → Indianapolis** is first.
- **Both warm →** Grand Rapids for **vanlife comfort + relationship access**;
  Indianapolis for **steadier market potential**.
