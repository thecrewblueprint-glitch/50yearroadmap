# 06 — Safety

**Status:** Partial (operating baseline set; detailed procedures build with `dh-11`).

Safety is core to Deadhang's promise ("safety-first live-event production
labor"). This document holds Deadhang's safety baseline and will grow into full
safety documentation as it is built.

## Operating baseline

Deadhang operates to **industry safety standards first — OSHA, ANSI, ESTA, and
ETCP** — **and** follows each venue's/client's site-specific safety
requirements. When the two differ, the higher industry standard takes priority.

- **OSHA** — general workplace safety (incl. the General Duty Clause).
- **ANSI / ESTA** — entertainment-industry rigging and event standards.
- **ETCP** — Entertainment Technician Certification Program *standards and
  concepts* are used as the rigging/safety reference. (Deadhang aligns to ETCP
  standards; it does not claim certifications it does not hold. Note: personal
  ETCP certification is an active owner goal.)
- **Site/client requirements** — always followed on top of the baseline.

## Planned contents

- **Safety philosophy** — safety-first culture, stop-work authority, "it's not
  heavy unless you lift it wrong."
- **General procedures** — load-in/out safety, staging, equipment handling,
  exclusion zones, PPE expectations.
- **Rigging safety** — hardware inspection (roundslings, shackles, master
  links), bridle/angle-tension awareness, supervised-rigging boundaries.
- **Hazard recognition** — three-zone assessment, show-flow risk lifecycle,
  predictive hazard recognition.
- **Standards alignment** — reference OSHA General Duty Clause, ASME/ANSI/ESTA,
  and ETCP concepts (without claiming certification Deadhang doesn't hold).
