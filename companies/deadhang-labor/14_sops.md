# 14 — Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

**Status:** Documented (living). The repeatable step-by-step procedures for how
Deadhang runs day to day. These make the work consistent whether the owner does it
or a future crew member does — and they are the operational backbone beneath the
narrative in doc 03 (Operations).

> Living document — update a procedure whenever the real process changes.
> Public-safe: no client names, rates, or PII. _Last updated: 2026-07-18._

## SOP-1 — Inquiry → booking

1. **Receive inquiry** (website contact form via Formspree → business email, or
   direct/staffing-company outreach).
2. **Qualify:** event type, dates, location, role needed, hours, travel/lodging
   expectations. Confirm it fits Deadhang's services (doc 02) and qualifications.
3. **Agree terms in writing:** scope, dates, rate basis, and — for travel work —
   that travel/lodging is arranged in advance (per the website terms).
4. **Paperwork:** send the Client MSA where a new client relationship warrants it;
   provide a W-9 to clients/staffing companies as needed (doc 05).
5. **Confirm the booking** (dates held) and add it to the tracker (SOP-6).

## SOP-2 — Pre-gig preparation

1. Confirm **call time, location, parking/load-in, point of contact, and dress/PPE**.
2. Verify **gear ready:** required PPE, basic tools, appropriate clothing (doc 06).
3. Confirm **role scope and any specialized-work boundaries** — only perform
   rigging/lifts/electrical where qualified and authorized (docs 05, 06).
4. For travel gigs: confirm **travel + lodging** arrangements are set.

## SOP-3 — On-site / show-day

1. **Arrive early**, check in with the crew lead, attend the safety briefing.
2. **Follow site + client safety rules** on top of the OSHA/ANSI/ESTA/ETCP
   baseline (doc 06); use PPE; keep exclusion zones.
3. **Work to the role**; respect the lead; keep communication open.
4. **Stop-work authority:** refuse or halt unsafe work; report hazards/incidents
   immediately.
5. **Track hours** as worked (start/stop, breaks) for the invoice.

## SOP-4 — Post-gig → invoice

1. **Log hours and details** in the tracker (SOP-6) same day while fresh.
2. **Generate the invoice** from the Master Invoice Template / 1099 generator
   (doc 04, Contractor Tools): line items, hours, rate, payment terms
   (Net 15/30/45/60 or Due on Receipt).
3. **Send** to the client/staffing company; record the send date and due date.
4. **File** a copy with business records (SOP-7).

## SOP-5 — Payment tracking → follow-up

1. Record **invoice status** (sent → due → paid) in the payments tracker (doc 02
   Contractor Tools; the "2026 Work, Contracts & Payments Master Tracker").
2. On/after the due date, **follow up** on unpaid invoices politely and promptly.
3. On payment, **mark paid** with date and method; reconcile against the invoice.
4. Flag disputes early; keep documentation.

## SOP-6 — Gig / contract / payment tracking

1. Maintain **one canonical tracker** (the "2026 Work, Contracts & Payments Master
   Tracker") — retire duplicates (doc 12).
2. Per gig, capture: date(s), client/staffing company, event, role, hours, rate
   basis, invoice #, invoice status, payment date, travel/expenses.
3. This is the single source for invoicing, follow-up, and year-end financials
   (P&L, taxes).

## SOP-7 — Recordkeeping & monthly close

1. **Keep supporting records:** invoices, payments, expense receipts, mileage,
   travel — organized by month (doc 04).
2. **Monthly:** reconcile the tracker against the bank account; enter the month's
   revenue and expenses into the P&L; file receipts.
3. **Keep it private:** financial records and PII stay in private storage, never
   in the public repo (doc 12, `DATA_SAFETY_POLICY.md`).
4. **Year-end:** the P&L feeds Schedule C; coordinate with the tax preparer
   (doc 11, `dh-8`).

## SOP-8 — Engaging a contractor (future — gated)

Applies **only once** Deadhang begins deploying others (roadmap `dh-3`/`dh-10`).
Do **not** engage anyone until the gates are met:

1. **Gates first:** the Master Independent Contractor Agreement is
   attorney-reviewed (doc 05, `dh-10`) **and** appropriate insurance is in place
   (doc 11, `dh-9`).
2. **Onboard:** signed IC Agreement, W-9, and direct-deposit/onboarding forms
   (doc 12) before any work or payment.
3. **Classify carefully:** treat as an independent contractor per the agreement;
   1099-NEC where required. (1099 misclassification is the #1 legal risk — doc 13.)
4. **Dispatch:** issue clear call sheets/work orders; the IC agreement's terms
   apply by reference.

## SOP-9 — Merch fulfillment (as `dh-12` launches)

1. **Before selling goods:** register for **AZ TPT** and set up sales-tax
   collection/remittance (doc 11) — services don't trigger it, tangible goods do.
2. Use the **print-on-demand** provider's workflow; account for its built-in tax
   handling.
3. Record merch revenue and COGS in the P&L separately from labor income.
