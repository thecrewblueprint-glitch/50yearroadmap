# 00 — Overview

**Status:** Documented.

## Mission

The Crew Blueprint is a **professional training resource for American live-event
field hands**, focused on **real-world job readiness** rather than certification,
built to **US industry standards** (OSHA / ANSI / ESTA / ETCP + US practice). It
teaches the skills a hand uses on the job, the context behind them, and how the
work fits into the larger production — *what* you're doing, *why*, and *how it
fits the bigger picture.*

**Ground-up, and first of its kind.** It starts at the **ground / field-hand**
level — general field work, taught from real lived experience — and grows toward
specialized disciplines later. The aim is to be the **first US-standard gamified
training** for the trade: a comparable platform exists (Tech Crew HQ) but it is
Australia-only, built to Australian standards. See `partnerships/research/01`.

## Who it serves

Working live-event production crew, across experience levels:

- **Beginners** entering the trade who need orientation and fundamentals.
- **Experienced technicians** deepening or broadening their craft.

It is built to grow with the worker — from first day on a call to specialized,
discipline-specific skill.

## What it is

- A **visual, workflow-based** learning system (see doc 01).
- An **orientation / job-readiness** platform grounded in real field experience.
- A library that will **expand across every major production discipline** over
  time (see doc 01 for the discipline scope).
- A body of **canonical course packets** that are the single source of truth for
  all content (see docs 03 and 07).

## What it is NOT (explicit non-goals)

- **Not a certifying body.** It does not certify, credential, or license anyone.
- **Not OSHA certification.** It aligns to OSHA and other industry standards; it
  does not issue them.
- **Not employer training.** It does not replace the training and authorization a
  worker must receive from their employer.
- **Not a substitute for supervised, authorized work.** Specialized tasks require
  employer authorization and hands-on training (see doc 02).

## Current stage

Foundation complete; **content-first**. The Crew Blueprint is no longer blocked
by software development. Delivery is decided: a **gamified, static, browser-based
experience — not an LMS** (doc 05). The priority is producing high-quality course
content and wrapping it in simple gamified modules, plus the governance and
documentation to support it long term (see docs 05 and 08).

## Where it sits in the ecosystem

Within the wider system, The Crew Blueprint is the **skill-development and
industry-knowledge layer** — it prepares better workers who can then be sourced
and deployed through Deadhang Labor and discovered through Production Atlas. Its
safety teaching and Deadhang's operating safety standard are deliberately kept
consistent (see doc 04 and `companies/deadhang-labor/06_safety.md`).
