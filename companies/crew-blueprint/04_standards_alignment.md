# 04 — Standards Alignment

**Status:** Documented.

The Crew Blueprint's safety and technical content is **aligned to recognized
industry standards**. Alignment means the material teaches to these standards and
concepts — it does **not** certify them or confer any credential (see doc 02).

## Standards referenced

- **OSHA** — general workplace safety, including the General Duty Clause;
  taught as **awareness**, not certification.
- **ASME / ANSI** — rigging and mechanical standards (e.g. hardware, load, and
  inspection concepts used in the rigging material).
- **ESTA** — entertainment-industry technical standards for live events.
- **ETCP** — Entertainment Technician Certification Program **standards and
  concepts** are used as the rigging/safety reference. The Crew Blueprint aligns
  to ETCP concepts; it is not ETCP and does not grant ETCP certification.

## How alignment shows up in content

- Rigging and safety courses (doc 03) are written **to** these standards —
  hardware ID and inspection, bridle math and angle tension, and predictive
  hazard recognition all reference the relevant OSHA/ASME/ANSI/ESTA/ETCP concepts.
- Safety callouts throughout lessons reflect standard practice, not house
  opinion.
- Where a standard governs, the content points the learner to it rather than
  substituting for it.

## Consistency with Deadhang Labor

Deadhang Labor operates to the **same baseline** — OSHA / ANSI / ESTA / ETCP
first, plus each site's requirements (`companies/deadhang-labor/06_safety.md`).
The Crew Blueprint teaches these standards; Deadhang follows them on the job.
Keeping the two aligned is deliberate: the training reflects the real operating
standard, and the operating standard draws on the teaching material rather than
duplicating it.
