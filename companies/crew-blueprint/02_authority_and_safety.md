# 02 — Authority & Safety (Legal Philosophy)

**Status:** Documented (the trust model is decided — roadmap `cb-1`).

This is the trust framework that governs everything The Crew Blueprint publishes.
Because the content is often safety-relevant, the boundaries of what it does and
does not claim are stated up front and applied to every course.

## What The Crew Blueprint is (positioning)

An **educational orientation and job-readiness resource** for live-event
production workers, aligned to recognized industry standards (see doc 04). It
teaches skills and context. It builds better-prepared workers.

## What it is NOT (the boundaries)

- **Not OSHA certification.** Alignment to OSHA standards is not the same as
  issuing an OSHA credential. The Crew Blueprint does not certify OSHA compliance.
- **Not employer training.** It does not replace the training, orientation, or
  authorization a worker must receive from an actual employer for a given job.
- **Not a certifying / credentialing body.** No certificates, licenses, or
  qualifications are conferred. Completing a course means a learner studied the
  material — nothing more, nothing that a jobsite should treat as authorization.

## Safety disclaimers (applied to content)

- Courses carry **safety disclaimers** making the above boundaries explicit.
- **Specialized work requires employer authorization and hands-on training.**
  The material orients and prepares; it never authorizes a worker to perform
  regulated or specialized tasks (e.g. rigging, working at height, energized
  systems) on its own. That authorization comes from the employer and qualified
  supervision.
- Safety content is taught as **awareness and best practice aligned to industry
  standards**, not as a substitute for a site's own safety program, which always
  governs on the job.

## Why this boundary matters

Getting the authority model right **before** scaling content is deliberate
(roadmap `cb-1`): it protects learners (no false sense of authorization),
protects the business (clear, honest positioning), and keeps the resource
credible with the industry. Every new course is written inside these lines.

## Relationship to Deadhang Labor

The Crew Blueprint = **teaching** (awareness, skills, standards). Deadhang Labor's
`06_safety.md` = the **operating standard the business actually follows** on the
job. They are kept consistent and cross-referenced, but they are different things:
one educates, the other governs live work.
